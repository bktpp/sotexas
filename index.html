TEXAS

Population: 29.53 million (2021)

Take a detailed look at the state here.

Texas is known for its vast landscapes and independent spirit. It boasts a rich and diverse history that stretches back thousands of years. Before European exploration, indigenous tribes such as the Caddo, Comanche, and Apache inhabited the region, leaving behind a legacy of intricate cultures and traditions. Spanish explorers arrived in the 16th century, establishing missions and settlements that laid the groundwork for Spanish colonial rule. In 1836, Texas declared independence from Mexico, sparking the iconic Battle of the Alamo and ultimately joining the United States in 1845 as the 28th state. Since then, Texas has played a pivotal role in shaping American history. From its prominence in the cattle industry to its important role in the oil boom of the 20th century. Today, Texas stands as a beacon of innovation and diversity, blending its rich heritage with a forward-thinking mindset that continues to influence the nation.
